SECTION 1. Title.

19

This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Arkansas Save

20

Adolescents from Experimentation (SAFE) Act".

SECTION 2. Legislative findings.

23

The General Assembly finds that:

24

(1) Arkansas has a compelling government interest in protecting

25

the health and safety of its citizens, especially vulnerable children;

26

(2)(A) Only a small percentage of the American population

27

experiences distress at identifying with their biological sex.

28

(B) According to the American Psychiatric Association,

29

"For natal adult males, prevalence ranges from 0.005% to 0.014%, and for

30

natal females, from 0.002% to 0.003%.";

31

(3) For the small percentage of children who are gender

32

nonconforming or experience distress at identifying with their biological

33

sex, studies consistently demonstrate that the majority come to identify with

34

their biological sex in adolescence or adulthood, thereby rendering most

35

physiological interventions unnecessary;

36

(4) Furthermore, scientific studies show that individuals

37

struggling with distress at identifying with their biological sex often have

38

already experienced psychopathology, which indicates these individuals should

39

be encouraged to seek mental health services to address comorbidities and

40

underlying causes of their distress before undertaking any hormonal or

41

surgical intervention;

42

(5) Even among people who have undergone inpatient gender

43

reassignment procedures, suicide rates, psychiatric morbidities, and

44

mortality rates remain markedly elevated above the background population;

45

(6)(A) Some healthcare providers are prescribing puberty-

46

blocking drugs, such as gonadotropin-releasing hormone analogues, in order to

47

delay the onset or progression of puberty in children who experience distress

48

at identifying with their biological sex.

49

(B) The prescribing of puberty-blocking drugs is being

50

done despite the lack of any long-term longitudinal studies evaluating the

51

risks and benefits of using these drugs for the treatment of such distress or

52

gender transition;

53

(7) Healthcare providers are also prescribing cross-sex hormones

54

for children who experience distress at identifying with their biological

55

sex, despite the fact that no randomized clinical trials have been conducted

56

on the efficacy or safety of the use of cross-sex hormones in adults or

57

children for the purpose of treating such distress or gender transition;

58

(8) The use of cross-sex hormones comes with serious known

59

risks, such as:

60

(A) For biological females:

61

(i) Erythrocytosis, which is an increase in red

62

blood cells;

63

(ii) Severe liver dysfunction;

64

(iii) Coronary artery disease, including heart

65

attacks;

66

(iv) Cerebrovascular disease, including strokes;

67

(v) Hypertension;

68

(vi) Increased risk of breast and uterine cancers;

69

and

70

(vii) Irreversible infertility; and

71

(B) For biological males:

72

(i) Thromboembolic disease, including blood clots;

73

(ii) Cholelithiasis, including gallstones;

74

(iii) Coronary artery disease, including heart

75

attacks;

76

(iv) Macroprolactinoma, which is a tumor of the

77

pituitary gland;

78

(v) Cerebrovascular disease, including strokes;

79

(vi) Hypertriglyceridemia, which is an elevated

80

level of tryglycerides in the blood;

81

(vii) Breast cancer; and

82

(viii) Irreversible infertility;

83

(9) Genital and nongenital gender reassignment surgeries are

84

generally not recommended for children, although evidence indicates referrals

85

for children to have such surgeries are becoming more frequent;

86

(10)(A) Genital gender reassignment surgery includes several

87

irreversible invasive procedures for males and females and involves the

88

alteration of biologically healthy and functional body parts.

89

(B) For biological males, surgery may involve:

90

(i) Genital reconstruction including penectomy,

91

which is the removal of the penis;

92

(ii) Orchiectomy, which is the removal of the

93

testicles;

94

(iii) Vaginoplasty, which is the construction of a

95

vagina-like structure, typically through a penile inversion procedure;

96

(iv) Clitoroplasty, which is the construction of a

97

clitoris-like structure; and

98

(v) Vulvoplasty, which is the construction of a

99

vulva-like structure.

100

(C) For biological females, surgery may involve:

101

(i) A hysterectomy or oophorectomy;

102

(ii) Reconstruction of the urethra;

103

(iii) Genital reconstruction including

104

metoidioplasty or phalloplasty, which is the construction of a penis-like

105

structure;

106

(iv) Vaginectomy, which is the removal of the

107

vagina;

108

(v) Scrotoplasty, which is the construction of a

109

penis-like and scrotum-like structure; and

110

(vi) Implantation of erection or testicular

111

prostheses;

112

(11) The complications, risks, and long-term care concerns

113

associated with genital gender reassignment surgery for both males and

114

females are numerous and complex;

115

(12)(A) Nongenital gender reassignment surgery includes various

116

invasive procedures for males and females and also involves the alteration or

117

removal of biologically normal and functional body parts.

118

(B) For biological males, this surgery may involve:

119

(i) Augmentation mammoplasty;

120

(ii) Facial feminization surgery;

121

(iii) Liposuction;

122

(iv) Lipofilling;

123

(v) Voice surgery;

124

(vi) Thyroid cartilage reduction;

125

(vii) Gluteal augmentation;

126

(viii) Hair reconstruction; and

127

(ix) Other aesthetic procedures.

128

(C) For biological females, this surgery may involve:

129

(i) A subcutaneous mastectomy;

130

(ii) Voice surgery;

131

(iii) Liposuction;

132

(iv) Lipofilling;

133

(v) Pectoral implants; and

134

(vi) Other aesthetic procedures;

135

(13)(A) It is an accepted principle of economics and public

136

policy that when a service or product is subsidized or reimbursed, demand for

137

that service or product is increased.

138

(B) Between 2015 and 2016, gender reassignment surgeries

139

increased by nearly twenty percent (20%) in the United States;

140

(14) It is of grave concern to the General Assembly that the

141

medical community is allowing individuals who experience distress at

142

identifying with their biological sex to be subjects of irreversible and

143

drastic nongenital gender reassignment surgery and irreversible, permanently

144

sterilizing genital gender reassignment surgery, despite the lack of studies

145

showing that the benefits of such extreme interventions outweigh the risks;

146

and

147

(15) The risks of gender transition procedures far outweigh any

148

benefit at this stage of clinical study on these procedures.

SECTION 3. Arkansas Code Title 20, Chapter 9, is amended to add an

151

additional subchapter to read as follows:

152

153

Subchapter 15 — Arkansas Save Adolescents from Experimentation (SAFE) Act

154

155

20-9-1501. Definitions.

156

As used in this subchapter:

157

(1) "Biological sex" means the biological indication of male and

158

female in the context of reproductive potential or capacity, such as sex

159

chromosomes, naturally occurring sex hormones, gonads, and nonambiguous

160

internal and external genitalia present at birth, without regard to an

161

individual's psychological, chosen, or subjective experience of gender;

162

(2) "Cross-sex hormones" means:

163

(A) Testosterone or other androgens given to biological

164

females in amounts that are larger or more potent than would normally occur

165

naturally in healthy biological sex females; and

166

(B) Estrogen given to biological males in amounts that are

167

larger or more potent than would normally occur naturally in healthy

168

biological sex males;

169

(3) "Gender" means the psychological, behavioral, social, and

170

cultural aspects of being male or female;

171

(4) "Gender reassignment surgery" means any medical or surgical

172

service that seeks to surgically alter or remove healthy physical or

173

anatomical characteristics or features that are typical for the individual's

174

biological sex, in order to instill or create physiological or anatomical

175

characteristics that resemble a sex different from the individual's

176

biological sex, including without limitation, genital or nongenital gender

177

reassignment surgery performed for the purpose of assisting an individual

178

with a gender transition;

179

(5) "Gender transition" means the process in which a person goes

180

from identifying with and living as a gender that corresponds to his or her

181

biological sex to identifying with and living as a gender different from his

182

or her biological sex, and may involve social, legal, or physical changes;

183

(6)(A) "Gender transition procedures" means any medical or

184

surgical service, including without limitation physician's services,

185

inpatient and outpatient hospital services, or prescribed drugs related to

186

gender transition that seeks to:

187

(i) Alter or remove physical or anatomical

188

characteristics or features that are typical for the individual's biological

189

sex; or

190

(ii) Instill or create physiological or anatomical

191

characteristics that resemble a sex different from the individual's

192

biological sex, including without limitation medical services that provide

193

puberty-blocking drugs, cross-sex hormones, or other mechanisms to promote

194

the development of feminizing or masculinizing features in the opposite

195

biological sex, or genital or nongenital gender reassignment surgery

196

performed for the purpose of assisting an individual with a gender

197

transition.

198

(B) "Gender transition procedures" do not include:

199

(i) Services to persons born with a medically

200

verifiable disorder of sex development, including a person with external

201

biological sex characteristics that are irresolvably ambiguous, such as those

202

born with 46 XX chromosomes with virilization, 46 XY chromosomes with

203

undervirilization, or having both ovarian and testicular tissue;

204

(ii) Services provided when a physician has

205

otherwise diagnosed a disorder of sexual development that the physician has

206

determined through genetic or biochemical testing that the person does not

207

have normal sex chromosome structure, sex steroid hormone production, or sex

208

steroid hormone action;

209

(iii) The treatment of any infection, injury,

210

disease, or disorder that has been caused by or exacerbated by the

211

performance of gender transition procedures, whether or not the gender

212

transition procedure was performed in accordance with state and federal law

213

or whether not funding for the gender transition procedure is permissible

214

under this subchapter; or

215

(iv) Any procedure undertaken because the individual

216

suffers from a physical disorder, physical injury, or physical illness that

217

would, as certified by a physician, place the individual in imminent danger

218

of death or impairment of major bodily function unless surgery is performed;

219

(7) "Genital gender reassignment surgery" means a medical

220

procedure performed for the purpose of assisting an individual with a gender

221

transition, including without limitation:

222

(A) Surgical procedures such as penectomy, orchiectomy,

223

vaginoplasty, clitoroplasty, or vulvoplasty for biologically male patients or

224

hysterectomy or ovariectomy for biologically female patients;

225

(B) Reconstruction of the fixed part of the urethra with

226

or without a metoidioplasty; or

227

(C) Phalloplasty, vaginectomy, scrotoplasty, or

228

implantation of erection or testicular prostheses for biologically female

229

patients;

230

(8) "Healthcare professional" a person who is licensed,

231

certified, or otherwise authorized by the laws of this state to administer

232

health care in the ordinary course of the practice of his or her profession;

233

(9) "Nongenital gender reassignment surgery" means medical

234

procedures performed for the purpose of assisting an individual with a gender

235

transition including without limitation:

236

(A) Surgical procedures for biologically male patients,

237

such as augmentation mammoplasty, facial feminization surgery, liposuction,

238

lipofilling, voice surgery, thyroid cartilage reduction, gluteal

239

augmentation, hair reconstruction, or various aesthetic procedures; or

240

(B) Surgical procedures for biologically female patients,

241

such as subcutaneous mastectomy, voice surgery, liposuction, lipofilling,

242

pectoral implants, or various aesthetic procedures;

243

(10) "Physician" means a person who is licensed to practice

244

medicine in this state;

245

(11) Puberty-blocking drugs" means gonadotropin-releasing

246

hormone analogues or other synthetic drugs used in biological males to stop

247

luteinizing hormone secretion and therefore testosterone secretion, or

248

ynthetic drugs used in biological females which stop the production of

249

estrogens and progesterone, when used to delay or suppress pubertal

250

development in children for the purpose of assisting an individual with a

251

gender transition; and

252

(12) "Public funds" means state, county, or local government

253

monies, in addition to any department, agency, or instrumentality authorized

254

or appropriated under state law or derived from any fund in which such moneys

255

are deposited.

256

20-9-1502. Prohibition of gender transition procedures for minors.

258

(a) A physician or other healthcare professional shall not provide

259

gender transition procedures to any individual under eighteen (18) years of

260

age.

261

(b) A physician, or other healthcare professional shall not refer any

262

individual under eighteen (18) years of age to any healthcare professional

263

for gender transition procedures.

264

(c) A physician or other healthcare professional is not prohibited

265

from providing any of the following procedures which are not gender

266

transition procedures to an individual under eighteen (18) years of age:

267

(1) Services to persons born with a medically verifiable

268

disorder of sex development, including a person with external biological sex

269

characteristics that are irresolvably ambiguous, such as those born with 46

270

XX chromosomes with virilization, 46 XY chromosomes with undervirilization,

271

or having both ovarian and testicular tissue;

272

(2) Services provided when a physician has otherwise diagnosed a

273

disorder of sexual development that the physician has determined through

274

genetic or biochemical testing that the person does not have normal sex

275

chromosome structure, sex steroid hormone production, or sex steroid hormone

276

action;

277

(3) The treatment of any infection, injury, disease, or disorder

278

that has been caused by or exacerbated by the performance of gender

279

transition procedures, whether or not the gender transition procedure was

280

performed in accordance with state and federal law or whether not funding for

281

the gender transition procedure is permissible under this subchapter; or

282

(4) Any procedure undertaken because the individual suffers from

283

a physical disorder, physical injury, or physical illness that would, as

284

certified by a physician, place the individual in imminent danger of death or

285

impairment of major bodily function unless surgery is performed.

286

287

20-9-1503. Prohibition on use of public funds for gender transition

288

procedures.

289

(a) Public funds shall not be directly or indirectly used, granted,

290

paid, or distributed to any entity, organization, or individual that provides

291

gender transition procedures to an individual under eighteen (18) years of

292

age.

293

(b) Healthcare services furnished in the following situations shall

294

not include gender transition procedures to an individual under eighteen (18)

295

years of age:

296

(1) By or in a healthcare facility owned by the state or a

297

county or local government; or

298

(2) By a physician or other healthcare professional employed by

299

state or a county or local government.

300

(c) Any amount paid by an individual or an entity during a taxable

301

year for provision of gender transition procedures or as premiums for health

302

care coverage that includes coverage for gender transition procedures is not

303

tax-deductible.

304

(d) The Arkansas Medicaid Program shall not reimburse or provide

305

coverage for gender transition procedures to an individual under eighteen

306

(18) years of age.

307

308

20-9-1504. Enforcement.

309

(a) Any referral for or provision of gender transition procedures to

310

an individual under eighteen (18) year of age is unprofessional conduct and

311

is subject to discipline by the appropriate licensing entity or disciplinary

312

review board with competent jurisdiction in this state.

313

(b) A person may assert an actual or threatened violation of this

314

subchapter as a claim or defense in a judicial or administrative proceeding

315

and obtain compensatory damages, injunctive relief, declaratory relief, or

316

any other appropriate relief.

317

(c)(1) A person shall bring a claim for a violation of this subchapter

318

no later than two (2) years after the day the cause of action accrues.

319

(2) An individual under eighteen (18) years of age may bring an

320

action throughout their minority through a parent or next friend, and may

321

bring an action in their own name upon reaching majority at any time from

322

that point until twenty (20) years after reaching the age of majority.

323

(d) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, an action under this

324

subchapter may be commenced, and relief may be granted, in a judicial

325

proceeding without regard to whether the person commencing the action has

326

sought or exhausted available administrative remedies.

327

(e) In any action or proceeding to enforce a provision of this

328

subchapter, a prevailing party who establishes a violation of this subchapter

329

shall recover reasonable attorneys' fees.

330

(f)(1) The Attorney General may bring an action to enforce compliance

331

with this subchapter.

332

(2) This subchapter does not deny, impair, or otherwise affect

333

any right or authority of the Attorney General, the State of Arkansas, or any

334

agency, officer, or employee of the state, acting under any law other than

335

this subchapter, to institute or intervene in any proceeding.

344
